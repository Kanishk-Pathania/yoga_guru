Overview of Yoga

Yoga derived from the Sankrit word "yuj" which means "to unite or integrate" yoga is a 5,000+ year old Indian body of knowledge. Yoga is all about harmonizing the body with the mind and breath through the means of various breathing exercises, yoga poses (asanas) and meditation. Though many think of yoga only as a physical exercise where people twist, turn, stretch, and breathe in the most complex ways, these are actually only the most superficial aspect of this profound science of unfolding the infinite potentials of the human mind and soul. The science of Yoga imbibe itself the complete essence of the Way of Life, including - Gyan Yoga or philosophy, Bhakti Yoga or path of devotional bliss, Karma Yoga or path of blissful action, and Raja Yoga or path of mind control. Raja Yoga is further divided into eight parts. At the heart of the Raja Yoga system, balancing and unifying these various approaches, is the practice of Yoga Asana. Yoga is an ancient practice that combines physical postures, breathing exercises, and meditation to promote overall well-being. It originated in India over 5,000 years ago and has since evolved into various styles and schools, each offering unique benefits.

The practice of yoga helps improve flexibility, strength, and balance. It also reduces stress, enhances mental clarity, and promotes emotional stability. Whether you are looking to improve your physical health, find inner peace, or connect with a community, yoga offers something for everyone.
